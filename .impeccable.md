## Design Context for Atlantic Pools

### Users
Atlantic Pools serves two main audiences:
- **Casual browsers**: Homeowners researching pools on mobile at night/weekends, gathering ideas and inspiration
- **Serious buyers**: Homeowners on desktop ready to get quotes, review pricing, and contact for services
- **Context**: The site is visited during casual research AND when buyers are ready to commit

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Competent, Reliable, Established

Atlantic Pools is not trendy or flashy—it's *proven*. The brand voice should communicate that this is a business that knows pools inside-out, has been doing this for 30+ years, and gets the job done right. Tone: straightforward, no-nonsense, professional. Buyers should feel confident they're hiring the best.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone**: Clean & Premium. Deep navy foundation with a warm gold/bronze accent instead of bright blue.

**Why**: Gold shifts the feel from "corporate tech" (bright blue) to "established expertise & value." It suggests luxury without being flashy, pairs beautifully with navy, and reads as timeless rather than trendy.

**Theme**: Light mode (white background, navy text, gold accents). Responsive to all screen sizes.

**Anti-references**:
- NO loud gradients or glowing effects
- NO decorative icons/emojis above every heading
- NO overly "designerly" or trendy details
- Aim for timeless, not chasing 2025 design fads

### Typography
**Font pairing**: Refined serif (display/headings) + clean sans (body/UI)

- **Display font** (headings, hero, prominent text): A distinctive serif that conveys "established" and "quality" without feeling dated. Should suggest craftsmanship and attention to detail.
- **Body font** (paragraphs, labels, UI): A refined, readable sans-serif for clarity and modern accessibility.

Replace the current DM Sans with fonts that reinforce the "competent professional" personality.

### Color Palette
- **Primary**: Navy (#0a2540) — trust, professionalism, depth
- **Accent**: Gold/bronze (to be selected for warmth and established luxury)
- **Neutrals**: White, off-white, soft grays (tinted toward navy)
- **Supporting**: Subtle grays for secondary text, borders, backgrounds

Avoid bright blue (#1565c0). Keep accents warm and sophisticated.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity first** — Every element serves a purpose. No decorative noise.
2. **Typography-driven** — Let the serif/sans pairing be the visual story. Clean spacing. Readable line lengths.
3. **Intentional hierarchy** — Use size, weight, and color to guide users' attention. No flat hierarchies.
4. **Timeless professionalism** — Design for 10 years, not 10 months. Avoid trends.
5. **Fast & responsive** — Motion is minimal and purposeful. Transitions are subtle.
